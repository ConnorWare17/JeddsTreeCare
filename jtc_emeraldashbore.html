The Emerald Ash Borer

What is an Emerald Ash Borer?

The Emerald Ash Bore is an invasive species accidentaly tranported to the United States from Asia. They were initially found near Detroit Michigan in 2002 and have spread through most of the US east of the Rockies and Eastern Canada. Their Larvae feed on tha bark of the treefrom the inside and disrupt the tree's ability to transport water and nutrients to the branches.

Things to know

  • They attack only Ash trees
  • They leave "D" shaped exit holes on the trees when they emerge
  • Woodpeckers eat EAB larvae, large numbers of woodpeckers and great damage from them may be a sign of EAB infestation
